NEW CADRE OF DISTRIBUTORS

Overview

The SEBI Circular dated September 13, 2012, introduced a new cadre of distributors allowed to sell simple and performing mutual fund schemes. This includes postal agents, retired government and semi-government officials, retired teachers, and other individuals with relevant experience, such as bank correspondents.

Additionally, intermediaries like insurance agents, FD agents, and business correspondents with at least 5 years of experience were also included. AMFI has finalized the registration process for these distributors, specifying eligibility criteria, required documents, and a self-certification declaration for applicants. This cadre is authorized to sell mutual fund schemes as defined in the SEBI circular.

Following are some important points to be noted:

The salient features of an ARN are:

NISM has introduced the Series-V-B: Mutual Fund Foundation Certification and CPE Programme for new distributors, as per SEBI's September 13, 2012 circular. Distributors needing an ARN must submit a self-attested copy of their NISM Series V-B or Mutual Fund Foundation CPE certificate.

While exempt from KYD/bio-metrics, they must provide self-attested identity and address proofs (like a photo PAN card), along with additional documents such as an experience certificate, relieving letter, and appointment letter.

Eligibility is limited to individuals or proprietary concerns that meet specific criteria. Once approved, distributors will receive a unique ARN starting with "SD."

Fees payable are as under:

Individual Category Fees

For applications received under Individual category : Rs. 1500/- for ARN Registration Rs. 750/- for ARN Renewal.

Proprietary Concern Fees

Fees for Proprietary Concern: Rs. 1500 for registration and EIJIN allotment, Rs. 750 for renewal.

According to the SEBI Circular from September 13, 2012, new distributors can sell simple and effective mutual fund schemes. AMCs must ensure these distributors comply with the Circular.

Fees were waived for new distributors from Feb I to Jun 30, 2013, subject to conditions.
