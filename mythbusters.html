SMARTOMORROWS MYTH BUSTERS

One needs a large sum to invest in Mutual Funds.

One does not necessarily need to invest a huge amount in Mutual Funds. One can start to invest in mutual funds with an amount as low as Rs. 500/-.

NFOs (new fund offers) are better than existing schemes

Existing schemes with proven track records are often better choices as they have demonstrated performance history and established portfolios.

You need to time MF investments

Regular, systematic investments are more effective than trying to time the market. Consistency is key in mutual fund investing.

Mutual fund investments are only for youngsters.

Mutual funds are suitable for all age groups, depending on their financial goals.

Mutual Funds are only for those who like to invest in the stock market

Mutual funds offer various investment options beyond stocks, including debt and hybrid funds, suitable for different risk appetites.

Buy a fund once it announces dividend.

Dividend announcements don't indicate better fund performance. The fund's NAV reduces by the dividend amount paid.

Performance of funds is directly related to the stock market

Fund performance depends on various factors including fund management, asset allocation, and investment strategy, not just market movements.

Choosing dividend pay-out or growth option is the same in all funds.

The choice between dividend pay-out and growth can significantly affect your returns.

Mutual Funds with lower NAV (Net Asset Value) are best to invest

NAV is simply the per-unit market value of a fund. A lower NAV doesn't necessarily mean better returns. Focus on the fund's performance, strategy, and alignment with your goals.

Redeem funds with high NAV and reinvest in schemes with low NAV

Redeeming funds based solely on NAV can be misleading. It's essential to consider the fund's performance, investment strategy, and alignment with your financial goals rather than just the NAV.

Debt funds are also impacted by equity market movement

Debt fund returns are primarily influenced by interest rate changes and credit quality, not equity market movements.

Mutual funds investments are riskier than stock investments.

Mutual funds can be less risky than individual stocks due to diversification.
